This project was for a Graphic Design Production class where we had to create a poster for a National Park of our choice. I decided to go with Lassen Volcanic National Park in Northern California known for its suffer pits, diverse forests and wildlife. With the design of this project I wanted to go with a vintage nature-travel magazine sort of look. I did this by choosing a warm-muted color scheme, stylistic typefaces, and many details.

Above is a project from an Interactive Design class where we were assigned to code a website based on the postmodern design movement, this could include a specific artist, company, project, or a broad idea of the time. I created my website based on the collage and graphics artist ‘Art Chantry’ as seen above, implementing a gallery of his work, interviews he has done, and designing the page to match his bold and out of the box work.

With the project above I had to create a booklet based on an old style, modern, and lineal font, for a Typography class. Above is one of the spreads I created, focusing on the modern typeface Dibot. I attempted to highlight the font using diagonal shapes and contrasting colors to show the change from bold lines to thin lines and the variety it provides.

Above is a digital view of my custom business cards, which I designed as a part of a Profession Development class I took. With the card I wanted to go with a more unique and abstract approach, leaning off my initials IF, as if it was a question hence the question mark. Another reason for the question marking as one of the main elements is that when working with others I like to ask questions often to generate more ideas and make sure we are all on the same page!

These are a collection of sticker designs I created for my job at the Oregon State Memorial Union Activities Team. I make a new sticker every month that is then ordered and given out to the student body to boost morale and bring a smile to the MU community. Every sticker is based on the school mascot, a beaver, with this specific beaver characterization I created. Through creating these stickers I have developed my illustration abilities, my idea generating and my connection with my campus.
